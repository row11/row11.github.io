Roie Levin

Google Scholar | Semantic Scholar | DBLP

I am a first second third fourth fifth (and final!) year PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University. I am in the Theory Group within the Computer Science Department, though nominally my degree is in Algorithms, Combinatorics and Optimization (ACO). I am very fortunate to be advised by Anupam Gupta. I am broadly interested in theoretical computer science, more specifically in approximation algorithms for uncertain environments (e.g. online/dynamic/streaming models). I also like pretty much anything involving submodular functions.

Previously I worked at the Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence from 2015 to 2017. I was a research engineer on the Euclid team.

Before that I received a B.Sc. in Computer Science/Applied Mathematics, and a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Brown University, class of 2015.
